1. 📈 Compound Interest Calculator

Compound interest is the eighth wonder of the world. Albert Einstein reportedly said that, and for good reason. A compound interest calculator shows you exactly how your investments grow over time — and the results are often surprising.

With this tool, you can:

  • Set your initial investment amount
  • Add monthly contributions
  • Adjust expected annual return (historically 7-10% for stocks)
  • Choose compounding frequency (daily, monthly, yearly)
  • See year-by-year growth with a visual chart
💡 Real example: A 25-year-old who invests $500/month with an 8% average return will have over $1.5 million by age 65. Starting just 10 years later at 35? That same $500/month yields only $745,000. The cost of waiting: $755,000.

Three Financial Rules Everyone Should Know

📐 The Rule of 72

To estimate how long it takes your money to double at a given return rate, divide 72 by the rate. At 8% return: 72 ÷ 8 = 9 years to double. At 12%: just 6 years.

💰 The 50/30/20 Budget

A simple budgeting framework: 50% of income for needs, 30% for wants, and 20% for savings and investments. Use the compound interest calculator to see what that 20% can become over time.

📊 The 4% Rule

A common retirement guideline: you can safely withdraw 4% of your retirement savings each year without running out of money. If you need $40,000/year in retirement, you'll need approximately $1,000,000 saved.
