Color Blindness

Color blindness affects approximately 1 in 12 men (8%) and 1 in 200 women. The most common types are deuteranopia (green deficiency) and protanopia (red deficiency). This test uses Ishihara-style plates — the same method used by eye doctors — to screen for color vision deficiency.

❓ Color Blindness FAQ

What are the 4 types of color blindness?

The four main types are: Deuteranopia (green-blind, most common), Protanopia (red-blind), Tritanopia (blue-blind, rare), and Achromatopsia (total color blindness, extremely rare). About 8% of men and 0.5% of women have some form.

Can color blindness be cured?

There is currently no cure for color blindness. However, special glasses and contact lenses can help some people distinguish colors better. Most people with color blindness adapt well and lead normal lives.
