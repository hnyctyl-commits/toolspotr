Mortgage Planning

Buying a home is the biggest purchase most people make. Understanding your monthly payment, total interest, and how different rates affect your costs is essential. A 1% rate difference on a $300,000 loan can save or cost you over $60,000 over 30 years.

❓ Mortgage Calculator FAQ

How much is a $300,000 mortgage per month?

At 6.5% interest for 30 years, a $300,000 mortgage costs approximately $1,896 per month. This includes principal and interest but not taxes and insurance.

What is the difference between 15-year and 30-year mortgage?

A 15-year mortgage has higher monthly payments but significantly lower total interest. A $300,000 loan at 6% for 15 years costs about $2,531/month with $155,000 total interest. The same loan for 30 years costs $1,799/month but $347,000 total interest.

How much house can I afford with a $5,000 monthly payment?

At current rates around 6.5%, a $5,000 monthly payment would allow you to afford approximately $790,000 with a 30-year loan, or $570,000 with a 15-year loan. Use this calculator to try different amounts and find your sweet spot.
